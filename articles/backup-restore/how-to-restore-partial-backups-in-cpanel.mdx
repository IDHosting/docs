<Note>Restoring a partial backup will overwrite your existing data. Ensure you have a full backup before proceeding.</Note>

## Restore Partial Backups

This guide will walk you through the process of restoring a partial backup using cPanel.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to cPanel">
    Begin by logging in to your cPanel account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Backup Wizard">
    In the **Files** section, click on **Backup Wizard**.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/backupwizard-icon.gif"
        alt="Backup Wizard icon in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Restore Option">
    Under the **Backup/Restore** section, click on **Restore**.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/restore-option.gif"
        alt="Restore option within Backup Wizard"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Restore Type">
    Under **Select Restore Type**, choose the appropriate option for your partial backup.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/paper-select-restore-type.gif"
        alt="Selecting restore type in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload Backup File">
    Click on **Browse** to locate the backup file on your computer. Select the file and then click on **Upload**.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/restore-upload.gif"
        alt="Uploading a backup file in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once the upload is complete, you should see a message confirming that your restore has been successful.
