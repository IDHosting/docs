---
title: "Modify a Database User's Privileges in DirectAdmin"
description: "This tutorial shows you how to modify the privileges of a database user in DirectAdmin."
---

This tutorial shows you how to modify the privileges of a database user in DirectAdmin.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your DirectAdmin Account">
    Begin by logging into your DirectAdmin control panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Databases">
    In the **Account Manager** section, click on **Databases**. Alternatively, you can use the **search bar** at the top of the page and type "Databases", then click on the result.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/da-databases-icon.gif" 
        alt="DirectAdmin Databases Icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manage Database">
    From the list of your databases, locate the one you wish to modify and click on the **Manage** option next to it.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/da-mysql-db-manage-option.gif" 
        alt="DirectAdmin MySQL Database Manage Option"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access User Privileges">
    Under the **User Access** section, you will see a list of your database users. Click on **Privileges** for the user whose permissions you want to change.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/da-db-user-privileges-navigation.gif" 
        alt="DirectAdmin Database User Privileges Navigation"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Modify and Save Changes">
    Adjust the user's privileges according to your requirements. Once you have made the necessary modifications, click on the **Save Changes** button.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/da-piv-modify-change.gif" 
        alt="DirectAdmin Modify Privileges and Save Changes"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your database user's privileges should now be updated successfully.
