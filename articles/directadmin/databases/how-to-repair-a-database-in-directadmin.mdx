---
title: "Repair a Database in DirectAdmin"
description: "Learn how to repair a corrupted or error-prone database using DirectAdmin."
---

This guide will walk you through the process of repairing a database in DirectAdmin, which is essential for resolving issues with corrupted tables or errors.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to DirectAdmin">
    Begin by logging into your DirectAdmin account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Databases">
    In the **Account Manager** section, click on **Databases**.
    <br />
    Alternatively, you can type **Databases** in the **search bar** and select the option when it appears.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/da-databases-icon.gif"
        alt="DirectAdmin Databases Icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manage the Database">
    From the list of your databases, locate the one you wish to repair and click on **Manage** next to it.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/da-mysql-db-manage-option.gif"
        alt="DirectAdmin MySQL Database Manage Option"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Repair the Database">
    Scroll down to the **Database Operations** section and click on the **Repair** button.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/da-mysql-db-repair.gif"
        alt="DirectAdmin MySQL Database Repair Button"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
    <Note>
      Upon successful repair, a report detailing the operation will be displayed.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>
