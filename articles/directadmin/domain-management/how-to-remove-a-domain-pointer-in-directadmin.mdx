---
title: "Remove a Domain Pointer in DirectAdmin"
description: "Learn how to remove a domain pointer in DirectAdmin to manage your domains effectively."
---

In this tutorial, you will learn how to remove a domain pointer in DirectAdmin. This is useful if you no longer need a pointer or want to clean up unused domains.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to DirectAdmin">
    Begin by logging into your DirectAdmin account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Domain Pointers">
    In the **Account Manager** section, click on **Domain Pointers**.
    Alternatively, you can type "Domain Pointers" into the search bar and select the option when it appears.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/da-domainpointer-icon.gif"
        alt="DirectAdmin Domain Pointers icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Domain Pointer for Removal">
    Under the **Domain Pointers** section, locate and select the specific domain pointer you wish to remove.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete and Confirm">
    Click the **Delete** button. A confirmation prompt will appear; confirm the deletion to proceed.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/da-domainpointer-remove.gif"
        alt="Confirming domain pointer deletion in DirectAdmin"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your domain pointer should now be successfully deleted.
