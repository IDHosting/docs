---
title: "Remove Sample Comments and Posts From WordPress"
description: "A guide on how to remove the default sample comments and posts from a fresh WordPress installation."
---

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to the WordPress Dashboard">
    Access your WordPress admin area by navigating to your website's login page (usually `yourdomain.com/wp-admin`) and entering your credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to All Posts">
    Once logged in, go to the **Posts** section in the left-hand dashboard menu and click on **All Posts**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/wp-first-time-all-post.gif" 
        alt="WordPress All Posts screen"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trash Sample Posts">
    Hover your mouse over the sample post you wish to remove. A **Trash** option will appear. Click on it to move the post to the trash.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/wp-ft-remove-post.gif" 
        alt="Trashing a sample post in WordPress"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trash Sample Pages">
    Next, navigate to the **Pages** section in the dashboard menu. Similar to posts, hover over the sample page and click **Trash**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/wp-ft-remove-page.gif" 
        alt="Trashing a sample page in WordPress"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trash Sample Comments">
    Finally, go to the **Comments** section in the dashboard. Hover over any sample comment and click **Trash** to remove it.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/wp-ft-remove-comment.gif" 
        alt="Trashing a sample comment in WordPress"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

Following these steps will ensure your WordPress installation is free of default sample content, providing a clean slate for your own posts and pages.
