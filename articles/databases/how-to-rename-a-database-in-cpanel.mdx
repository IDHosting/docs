---
title: "Rename a Database in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to rename a database directly within your cPanel interface."
---

This guide will walk you through the process of renaming a database using cPanel.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to cPanel">
    Access your cPanel account by navigating to your cPanel login URL and entering your credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Databases">
    Once logged in, locate the **Databases** section and click on **Manage My Databases**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/mysql-databases-icon.gif" 
        alt="cPanel MySQL Databases Icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate and Rename Database">
    Under the **Current Databases** list, find the database you wish to rename and click the **Rename** option next to it.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/rename-db.gif" 
        alt="Locating the Rename option for a database in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter New Database Name">
    In the provided field, enter the new desired name for your database. Then, click **Proceed** to confirm the change.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/change-dbname.gif" 
        alt="Entering the new database name and proceeding"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

This process allows you to easily manage and update your database names within cPanel.
