---
title: "Create a Database Username in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to create a database username in cPanel to connect your website or application to your database."
---

A database username is essential for connecting your website or application to your database. This guide will walk you through the process of creating one in cPanel.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to cPanel">
    Begin by logging into your cPanel account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Database Management">
    In the **Databases** section, click on **Manage My Databases**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/mysql-databases-icon.gif" 
        alt="Manage My Databases icon in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Username">
    Under the **Add New User** section, enter your desired username in the **Username** field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Password">
    Enter your new password in the designated field, or utilize the password generator for a strong, unique password.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/createdb-user.gif" 
        alt="Creating a new database user in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create User">
    Click on the **Create a User** button to finalize the process.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>It's recommended to write down the database username and password in a secure place, such as a notepad, for easy recall when manually installing scripts or applications.</Tip>

By following these steps, you have successfully learned how to create a database username in cPanel.
