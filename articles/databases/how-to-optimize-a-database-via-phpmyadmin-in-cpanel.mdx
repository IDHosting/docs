---
title: "Optimize a Database via phpMyAdmin in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to optimize your database using phpMyAdmin in cPanel to free up unused space and improve performance."
---

Optimizing your database in cPanel using phpMyAdmin is a straightforward process that can significantly boost performance by reclaiming unused space.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to cPanel">
    Begin by logging into your cPanel account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access phpMyAdmin">
    Once logged in, navigate to the **Databases** section and click on the **phpMyAdmin** icon.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/phpmyadmin-icon.gif"
        alt="phpMyAdmin icon in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Expand Database Navigation">
    In the left sidebar, click the **Expand icon** to reveal the database navigation tree.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/phpmyadmin-expand-db.gif"
        alt="Expanding database navigation in phpMyAdmin"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optimize Tables">
    Scroll down the list of tables, click on **Check All** to select all tables. Then, from the drop-down menu, choose **Optimize tables** and click to execute the action.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/optimize-db.gif"
        alt="Selecting 'Optimize tables' in phpMyAdmin"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
    <Note>Upon successful completion, you will see a confirmation message indicating that the table(s) have been optimized.</Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>
