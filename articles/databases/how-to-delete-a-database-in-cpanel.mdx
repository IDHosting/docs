<Note>
  Before deleting a database, ensure you have a recent backup. Once a database is deleted, it cannot be recovered without a backup.
</Note>

## Deleting a Database

Follow these steps to delete a database in your cPanel account:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your cPanel account">
    Access your cPanel account using your provided credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Database Management">
    In the **Databases** section, click on **Manage My Databases**.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/mysql-databases-icon.gif"
        alt="cPanel MySQL Databases Icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate and Delete the Database">
    Under the **Current Databases** list, find the database you wish to remove and click the **Delete** link next to it.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/delete-db.gif"
        alt="Delete Database Link in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Deletion">
    A confirmation prompt will appear. Click on **Delete Database** to finalize the removal.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/db-remove-confirmation.gif"
        alt="Database Removal Confirmation in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

By following these steps, you have successfully deleted a database from your cPanel account.
