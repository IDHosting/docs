---
title: "Create an Alias or Park a Domain in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to create an alias (formerly parked domain) in cPanel to mirror your primary website content."
---

An **Alias domain**, formerly known as a **parked domain**, allows you to create a mirror of your primary domain. Visitors using the alias domain will see the same content as your main website.

<Note>The domain you intend to use as an alias must have its DNS records pointed to our server.</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to cPanel">
    Begin by logging in to your cPanel account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Domains">
    In the **Domains** section, click on **Domains**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/cp-domains-icon.gif" 
        alt="cPanel Domains icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a New Domain">
    Click on **Create A New Domain**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/cp-create-a-new-domain.gif" 
        alt="Create A New Domain button in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Alias Domain">
    In the **Domain** field, enter the domain name you wish to set as an alias. Ensure that the **Share document root** option is selected.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/cp-create-an-alias-domain.gif" 
        alt="cPanel form for creating an alias domain with Share document root selected"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click on **Submit** to finalize the creation of your alias domain.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Congratulations! You should now see a confirmation message indicating that your alias domain has been successfully created.
