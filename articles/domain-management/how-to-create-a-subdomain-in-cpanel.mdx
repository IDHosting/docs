---
title: "Create a Subdomain in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to create a subdomain in your cPanel account to organize your website content."
---

A subdomain is an additional part of your primary domain, allowing you to create distinct sections of your website. For example, `blog.example.com` is a subdomain. Follow this tutorial to create a subdomain in cPanel.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to cPanel">
    Begin by logging into your cPanel account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Domains">
    In the **Domains** section, click on the **Domains** icon.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/cp-domains-icon.gif" 
        alt="cPanel Domains Icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a New Domain">
    Click on the **Create A New Domain** button.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/cp-create-a-new-domain.gif" 
        alt="Create A New Domain Button"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Domain Details">
    Under the **Create a New Domain** section, enter the following details:
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Domain:</strong> Enter your main domain name. This will be the base for your subdomain.</li>
      <li><strong>Share document root:</strong> Ensure this option is <strong>unchecked</strong> to create your subdomain correctly.</li>
      <li><strong>Subdomain:</strong> This field usually fills automatically. Remove the domain extension (e.g., `.com`, `.net`) to set your desired subdomain name.</li>
    </ul>
    Leave all other options as they are.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/addon-domain-form.gif" 
        alt="Addon Domain Form with subdomain details"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click the **Submit** button to create your subdomain.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You should see a success message confirming that your subdomain has been created.
