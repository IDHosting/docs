---
title: "Redirect a Subdomain to an External URL"
description: "Learn how to redirect a subdomain to an external URL using cPanel."
---

Redirecting a subdomain to an external URL is useful when you want visitors to be automatically sent to another website. In cPanel, you can set up a redirection in just a few steps.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to cPanel">
    Log in to your cPanel account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Redirects">
    In the **Domains** section, click on **Redirects**.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/cpanel-redirect.gif"
        alt="cPanel Redirects interface"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the Redirect">
    Under **Add Redirect**, enter the following details and choose the appropriate options:
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Type:</strong> Choose either <strong>Permanent (301)</strong> or <strong>Temporary (302)</strong>.</li>
      <li><strong>https?://(www.)?:</strong> Select the Subdomain you wish to redirect. You can also redirect a specific page within the subdomain.</li>
      <li><strong>Redirects to:</strong> Enter the external URL where you want to redirect your subdomain.</li>
    </ul>
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/redirect-addon.gif"
        alt="Add Redirect configuration in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the Redirect">
    Click on the **Add** button to save your redirect configuration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Congratulations! You have successfully created a redirect for your subdomain.
