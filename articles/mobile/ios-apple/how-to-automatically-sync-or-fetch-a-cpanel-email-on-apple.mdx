---
title: "Automatically Sync or Fetch a cPanel Email on Apple/iOS"
description: "Learn how to configure your Apple/iOS device to automatically sync new emails from your cPanel account."
---

You can set your email for automatic syncing. This means that if you receive new mail in your cPanel email account, it will be fetched and synced with your Apple/iOS mobile automatically.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    On your Apple device's main screen, tap on the **Settings** icon.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/ios-apple-home-screen.gif" 
        alt="Apple iOS Home Screen"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Passwords & Accounts">
    Scroll down and tap on **Passwords & Accounts**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/ios-apple-passwords-and-accounts.gif" 
        alt="Apple iOS Passwords & Accounts Screen"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Fetch New Data">
    Now tap on **Fetch New Data**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/fetch-apple-email.gif" 
        alt="Apple iOS Fetch New Data Screen"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Sync Frequency">
    Select an option such as **Every 15 Minutes**, or manually select an option and set your custom time to sync emails with your cPanel email server frequently. If any new email arrives, a new message will show up in your inbox.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/fech-select-apple.gif" 
        alt="Apple iOS Fetch New Data Options"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

Now, your email will be automatically synced/fetched with your iOS/iPhone.
