---
title: "Change an Email Account's Password in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to change your email account's password using cPanel with these step-by-step instructions."
---

You can change your email account's password through cPanel by following these steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your cPanel account">
    Access your cPanel dashboard by navigating to your cPanel URL and entering your credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Email Accounts">
    In the **Email** section, click on **Email Accounts**.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/email-account-icon.gif"
        alt="Email Accounts icon in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate your email account">
    Under the **Account @ domain** section, find the email account you wish to modify.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manage the email account">
    If your email address is `server@example.com`, click on **Manage** next to it.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/email-password-change-configration.gif"
        alt="Manage button for an email account"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a new password">
    In the **New Password** field, enter your desired new password. You can also use the password generator for a strong, secure password.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/email-password-change-final-configuration.gif"
        alt="New Password field in cPanel email settings"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update email settings">
    Scroll down to the bottom of the page and click on **Update Email Settings**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Congratulations, your email password has been successfully changed! You can now access your email using your new password.
