---
title: "Create an Email Autoresponder When You Are on Vacation"
description: "Learn how to set up an automatic email reply when you're on vacation or unavailable."
---

If you are on holiday or will be unavailable for an extended period, you can create an auto-reply for your emails. This ensures that anyone who emails you will receive an automated response with your predefined message.

## Setting Up an Autoresponder

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your cPanel account">
    Access your hosting control panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Autoresponders">
    In the **Email** section, click on **Autoresponders**.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/autoresponder-email-icon.gif"
        alt="Autoresponder email icon in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a New Autoresponder">
    From the Autoresponders page, click on **Add Autoresponder**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Autoresponder Details">
    Enter the following information:
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Interval:</strong> Set the number of hours the autoresponder should wait before sending a response to the same email address again.</li>
      <li><strong>Email:</strong> Enter the email address for which you want to enable the autoresponder (e.g., `support` for `support@example.com`).</li>
      <li><strong>Domain:</strong> Select the appropriate domain from the dropdown if you manage multiple domains.</li>
      <li><strong>From:</strong> Specify the name that will appear as the sender of the auto-response.</li>
      <li><strong>Subject:</strong> Enter a subject line for your auto-reply, such as "I am on vacation".</li>
      <li><strong>Body:</strong> Compose the main content of your automatic email reply.</li>
    </ul>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Schedule">
    Choose the start and stop dates/times for your autoresponder to be active.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/autoresponder-email-configuration.gif"
        alt="Autoresponder email configuration settings"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the Autoresponder">
    Click on the **Create** button to save your settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Removing an Autoresponder

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Autoresponders">
    In the **Email** section of your cPanel, click on **Autoresponders**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View Current Autoresponders">
    Your configured autoresponders will be listed under **Current Autoresponders**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete Autoresponder">
    Click on the **Delete** button located to the right of the email address you wish to remove the autoresponder from.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/autoresponder-email-delete.gif"
        alt="Delete autoresponder button"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Deletion">
    Confirm your action by clicking **Delete Autoresponder**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You have now learned how to set up and remove an email autoresponder.
