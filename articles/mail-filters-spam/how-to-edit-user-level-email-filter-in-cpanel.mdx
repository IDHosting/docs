---
title: "Edit User Level Email Filter in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to edit existing user-level email filters in cPanel to manage incoming emails more effectively."
---

Need to change how emails are filtered for a specific email account? Editing a user-level email filter in cPanel lets you update rules to better manage your inbox.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to cPanel">
    Access your cPanel account by logging in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Email Filters">
    In the **Email** section, click on **Email Filters**.
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/kb/user-level-filtering-icon.gif" alt="Email Filters icon in cPanel" style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }} />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select User and Manage Filters">
    Under **Filters by Users**, locate the email account you wish to manage and click on **Manage Filters** next to it.
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/kb/manage-filter-by-user-level.gif" alt="Manage Filters option for a user" style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }} />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit the Filter">
    Under **Current Filters**, find the filter you want to modify and click on **Edit** next to it.
    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/kb/edit-filter.gif" alt="Edit button for an existing filter" style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }} />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make Changes">
    Adjust the filter rules according to your needs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Click on the **Save** button to apply your modifications.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You have now successfully edited the user-level email filter in cPanel. Your new rules will help organize incoming emails according to your preferences.
