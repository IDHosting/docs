---
title: "Delete an Account Level/Global Email Filter in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to easily delete an account-level or global email filter in cPanel when it's no longer needed."
---

If you no longer need a specific spam filter, deleting an account level or global email filter in cPanel is simple. This helps keep your email settings clean and easy to manage.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your cPanel account">
    Access your cPanel dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Global Email Filters">
    In the **Email** section, click on **Global Email Filters**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/account-level-filtering-icon.gif" 
        alt="cPanel Global Email Filters icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate and Delete the Filter">
    Under **Current Filters**, all existing filters are displayed. Click **Delete** next to the filter you wish to remove.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/delete-filter.gif" 
        alt="Delete button next to an existing filter"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Deletion">
    Click on **Delete Filter** to confirm and remove your filter.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You have now successfully deleted the global email filter in cPanel, and it will no longer apply to your email accounts.
