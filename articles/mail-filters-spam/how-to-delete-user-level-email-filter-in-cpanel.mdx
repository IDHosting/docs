---
title: "Delete User Level Email Filter in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to remove an email filter for a specific account in cPanel."
---

If an email filter is no longer needed for a specific account, you can easily delete the user-level email filter in cPanel. This helps keep your filtering rules organized and relevant.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to cPanel">
    Access your cPanel account by logging in with your credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Email Filters">
    In the **Email** section, click on **Email Filters**.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/user-level-filtering-icon.gif"
        alt="Email Filters icon in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manage Filters for the User">
    Under **Filters by Users**, locate the email account for which you want to remove a filter. Click on **Manage Filters** next to that account.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/manage-filter-by-user-level.gif"
        alt="Manage Filters option for a user account"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete the Specific Filter">
    Under **Current Filters**, find the filter you wish to remove and click on the **Delete** button next to it.

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/delete-filter.gif"
        alt="Delete button for an email filter"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Deletion">
    A confirmation prompt will appear. Click on **Delete Filter** to permanently remove the filter.

    <Note>
      Once a filter is deleted, it will no longer affect incoming emails for that account.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

You have successfully deleted the user-level email filter in cPanel. This filter will no longer be applied to incoming emails for the specified account.
