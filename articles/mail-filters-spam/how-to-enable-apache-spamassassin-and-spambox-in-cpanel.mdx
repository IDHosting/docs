---
title: "Enable Apache SpamAssassin and SpamBox in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to enable Apache SpamAssassin and SpamBox in cPanel to filter spam emails effectively."
---

Apache SpamAssassin is a powerful mail filter that identifies and isolates spam. It uses a variety of tests to detect unsolicited bulk email by examining email headers and content with advanced statistical methods.

This guide will walk you through enabling Apache SpamAssassin and SpamBox in your cPanel account.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your cPanel Account">
    Access your cPanel account using your provided credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Spam Filters">
    In the **Email** section of your cPanel dashboard, click on **Spam Filters**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/apache-spamassassin-icon.gif" 
        alt="Apache SpamAssassin Icon in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Apache SpamAssassin">
    To enable Apache SpamAssassin, toggle the **Process New Emails and Mark them as Spam** option. If the toggle is greyed out, it means Apache SpamAssassin is currently disabled.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/apache-spam-assassin-enable-button.gif" 
        alt="Enable Apache SpamAssassin Button"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable SpamBox">
    Activate **SpamBox** by clicking on the **Move New Spam to a Separate Folder (Spam Box)** option.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/enable-spambox-cpanel.gif" 
        alt="Enable SpamBox in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once enabled, SpamAssassin will automatically move incoming spam messages with a spam score of 5 or higher to your Spam Box folder.
