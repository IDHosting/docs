---
title: "Disable Rescue Mode in Virtualizor"
description: "A guide on how to disable rescue mode for your Virtualizor VPS."
---

This guide will walk you through the steps to disable rescue mode on your Virtualizor VPS if you previously enabled it and need to revert to normal operation.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to Virtualizor">
    Access your Virtualizor control panel by logging in with your credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to List VPS">
    On the left-hand side panel, click on **List VPS**.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/virtualizor-list-vps-menu.gif"
        alt="Virtualizor List VPS menu"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manage VPS">
    Locate the VPS you wish to manage. Hover your mouse over the VPS row until it is highlighted, then click on the **Wrench icon** that appears on that line.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/virtualizor-click-manage-vps.gif"
        alt="Virtualizor click manage VPS icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access Rescue Mode Settings">
    In the management options, click on the **Rescue Mode** tab.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/virtualizor-rescue-mode-tab.gif"
        alt="Virtualizor Rescue Mode tab"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Disable Rescue Mode">
    Click the **Disable Rescue Mode** button.
    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/kb/virtualizor-disable-rescue-mode.gif"
        alt="Virtualizor disable Rescue Mode button"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your VPS will now reboot and start in its normal operating mode.
