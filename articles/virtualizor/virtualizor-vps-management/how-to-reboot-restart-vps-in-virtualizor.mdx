---

You can restart your Virtual Private Server (VPS) through the Virtualizor control panel or via the command line. This guide covers both methods.

## How to Reboot/Restart a VPS via Virtualizor?

Follow these steps to restart your VPS using the Virtualizor interface:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to Virtualizor">
    Access your Virtualizor Control Panel by logging in with your credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to List VPS">
    On the left-hand side panel, click on **List VPS**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/virtualizor-list-vps-menu.gif" 
        alt="Virtualizor List VPS menu"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manage VPS">
    Hover your mouse over the row of the VPS you wish to manage. The row will highlight. Click on the **Wrench icon** that appears on the highlighted row.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/virtualizor-click-manage-vps.gif" 
        alt="Virtualizor click manage VPS"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Restart VPS">
    In the top-right corner of the VPS management interface, click on the **Restart icon**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/virtualizor-restart-vps-icon.gif" 
        alt="Virtualizor restart VPS icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>The VPS restart process may take a few moments to complete. A confirmation message will be displayed once it's done.</Note>

## How to Reboot/Restart VPS via Command Line?

Alternatively, you can restart your VPS using SSH and a command.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in via SSH">
    Connect to your VPS using your preferred SSH client.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Execute Restart Command">
    Enter the following command and press Enter:
    ```bash
    shutdown -r now
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>After executing the command, your VPS will begin its reboot sequence, which may take some time.</Note>
