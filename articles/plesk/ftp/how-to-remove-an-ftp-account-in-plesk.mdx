---
title: "Remove an FTP Account in Plesk"
description: "Learn how to remove unused FTP accounts in Plesk to manage your hosting environment efficiently."
---

If you have created too many FTP user accounts and some are no longer in use, you can remove them by following these steps in Plesk.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your Plesk account">
    Access your Plesk control panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Websites & Domains">
    Click on **Websites & Domains** from the sidebar menu.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/plesk-websites-and-domains-menu.gif" 
        alt="Plesk Websites & Domains menu item"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your domain">
    Under the list of **Domain names**, click on the appropriate domain name.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/plesk-click-on-domain.gif" 
        alt="Clicking on a domain name in Plesk"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access FTP settings">
    From the **Dashboard**, under the **Files & Databases** section, click on **FTP**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/plesk-ftp-icon.gif" 
        alt="Plesk FTP icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove FTP accounts">
    Check the box next to the FTP accounts you wish to remove, and then click the **Remove** button.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/plesk-remove-ftp-account.gif" 
        alt="Selecting and removing FTP accounts in Plesk"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm removal">
    Confirm your action by clicking **Yes** to remove the selected FTP accounts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The FTP account(s) will now be successfully removed from your Plesk account.
