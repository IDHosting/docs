---
title: "Encrypt Database Password in Plesk Backup"
description: "Learn how to encrypt your database password within Plesk backups for enhanced security."
---

This tutorial will guide you through the process of encrypting your database password in Plesk backups.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to Plesk">
    Begin by logging into your Plesk account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Backup & Restore">
    Go to **Websites & Domains**, click on the **Pull icon**, and then select **Backup & Restore**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/plesk-account-backup-manager-access.gif" 
        alt="Accessing Backup & Restore in Plesk"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access Remote Storage Settings">
    Click on **Remote Storage Settings**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/click-remote-storage-settings.gif" 
        alt="Clicking on Remote Storage Settings"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Password Protection">
    Locate **Password Protection**. If it displays **Disabled**, click on **change**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/password-protect-backup-in-plesk.gif" 
        alt="Enabling Password Protection in Plesk"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Your Password">
    Check the box for **Use password protection** and enter your desired password. Confirm the password and then click **Apply**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/enable-password-protection-in-plesk-backup.gif" 
        alt="Setting a password for backup protection in Plesk"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Ensure you store your password in a secure location, as it is required for restoring your backup. If you forget your password or do not set one, Plesk will restore your backup with a randomly generated database password.
</Note>
