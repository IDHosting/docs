---
title: "Generate a Full Backup in Plesk"
description: "Learn how to create a full backup of your website using Plesk."
---

Backing up your website regularly is crucial for data safety. This guide will walk you through the process of generating a full backup of your website using Plesk.

## How to Generate a Backup of Your Plesk Account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your Plesk account">
    Access your Plesk control panel by logging in with your credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Backup & Restore">
    Go to **Websites & Domains**, click on the **Pull icon**, and then select **Backup & Restore**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/plesk-account-backup-manager-access.gif" 
        alt="Plesk Account Backup Manager Access"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Initiate a Backup">
    Under the **Backup Manager**, click the **Back Up** button.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/plesk-backup-manager-generate.gif" 
        alt="Plesk Backup Manager Generate Button"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Backup Settings">
    Select **Full** as the backup type. For the **Store in** option, choose **FTP(s)**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/plesk-backup-full-option.gif" 
        alt="Plesk Backup Full Option Configuration"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optional: Enable Notifications">
    If you want to be notified when the backup task is completed, check the box for "When a backup task is completed, send a notification email to".
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/plesk-backup-generate-enable-notification.gif" 
        alt="Plesk Backup Generate Enable Notification"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and Generate">
    Click **OK** to confirm your settings. The backup process will begin in the background, and you can navigate away from the page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How to Download a Backup of Your Plesk Account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Backup & Restore">
    Go to **Websites & Domains**, click on the **Pull icon**, and then select **Backup & Restore**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate and Download Backup">
    In the list of backups, find the backup you wish to download and click the **download icon**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/plesk-account-backup-download-icon.gif" 
        alt="Plesk Account Backup Download Icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optional: Password Protect Backup">
    If you wish to password protect the backup file, check the **Use password protection** option, enter your desired password, and then click **OK**.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/plesk-account-backup-password-protection.gif" 
        alt="Plesk Account Backup Password Protection"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save Backup File">
    Your browser will prompt you to download the backup file. Save it to a secure location on your computer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You have now successfully generated and downloaded a full backup of your Plesk account. Remember to store your backup files safely for future restoration needs.
