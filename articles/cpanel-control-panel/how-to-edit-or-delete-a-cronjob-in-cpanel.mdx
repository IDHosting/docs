---
title: "Edit or Delete a Cronjob in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to easily edit or delete cron jobs directly from your cPanel interface."
---

This guide will walk you through the process of modifying existing cron jobs or removing them entirely from your cPanel account.

## Editing a Cronjob

Follow these steps to edit an existing cron job:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to cPanel">
    Access your cPanel account using your provided credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Cron Jobs">
    In the **Advanced** section of your cPanel dashboard, click on the **Cron Jobs** icon.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/cronjob-icon.gif" 
        alt="cPanel Cron Jobs Icon"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate the Cronjob">
    Scroll down to the **Current Cron Jobs** section. Find the specific cron job you wish to edit and click the **Edit** button next to it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make Changes and Save">
    Modify the desired settings for your cron job. Once you have made your changes, click on the **Edit Line** button to save them.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/cronjob-edit.gif" 
        alt="Editing a Cronjob in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Deleting a Cronjob

To remove a cron job, follow these simple steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the Cronjob">
    In the **Current Cron Jobs** section, find the cron job you want to delete and click the **Delete** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Deletion">
    A confirmation prompt will appear. Click **Delete** again to confirm the removal of the cron job.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/delete-cronjob.gif" 
        alt="Confirming Cronjob Deletion in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

You have now successfully edited or deleted a cron job in cPanel.
