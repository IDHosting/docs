---
title: "Enable or Disable PHP's display_errors via CloudLinux Selector in cPanel"
description: "Learn how to enable or disable PHP's display_errors setting using the CloudLinux Selector in cPanel for debugging or security purposes."
---

<Note>
  Sometimes, you need to enable or disable PHP's <code>display_errors</code> setting for debugging or security reasons. You can easily manage this using the CloudLinux Selector within your cPanel account.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to your cPanel account">
    Access your cPanel account through your hosting provider's provided URL.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Select PHP Version">
    In the **Software** section of your cPanel dashboard, click on the **Select PHP Version** icon.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/cp-cl-select-php-version-icon.gif" 
        alt="Select PHP Version icon in cPanel"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access the Options Menu">
    Once on the PHP Selector page, click on the **Options** menu.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/cl-options-menu.gif" 
        alt="Options menu in CloudLinux PHP Selector"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
    <Warning>
      If the **Options** page displays an error related to the native PHP version, you must first change it to a non-native version before proceeding.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable or Disable display_errors">
    To enable <code>display_errors</code>, place a checkmark next to it. To disable it, uncheck the box.
    <Frame>
      <img 
        src="/images/kb/cl-enable-or-disable-error.gif" 
        alt="Enable or disable display_errors option"
        style={{ width: '100%', height: 'auto' }}
      />
    </Frame>
    <Note>
      After making your selection, a confirmation message will appear indicating whether <code>display_errors</code> has been enabled or disabled.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>
